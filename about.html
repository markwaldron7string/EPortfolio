About Me

I build fast, modern web applications that are designed to perform—and built to last.

I’m a frontend developer with a background that didn’t start in tech—but in music.

I’ve been playing guitar for over 20 years, focusing on melodic and technical styles that demand precision and discipline. That mindset carried over naturally into software development, where I approach building interfaces the same way I approach writing music: with intention, structure, and attention to detail.

Before transitioning into tech, I built a career in the insurance industry, moving from sales into underwriting at Progressive. That experience sharpened my analytical thinking, problem-solving, and ability to navigate complex systems—skills I now apply directly to building reliable, user-focused applications.

My shift into frontend development started as a search for a more creative and technical challenge. Even before coding, I was already deep into problem-solving—programming MIDI-controlled guitar processors, designing automated live setups, and building custom monitoring systems.

After completing a frontend-focused bootcamp, I built a full-featured application for Skinstric—an AI-powered skincare tool that uses the selfie camera to analyze users and generate personalized product recommendations.

That project was a turning point: it proved I could take an idea from concept to a fully functional product.

Today, I focus on building clean, responsive, high-performance web experiences using React and Next.js. I enjoy turning ideas into polished products and refining the details that make interfaces feel seamless.

I’m drawn to work that blends technical precision with creative expression—and frontend development sits right at that intersection.
