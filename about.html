About Me

I create fast, modern web applications with a focus on performance, usability, and clean design.

I’m a frontend developer with a background that didn’t start in tech—but in music.

I’ve been playing guitar for over 20 years, focusing on melodic and technical styles that demand precision, discipline, and attention to detail. That mindset naturally carried over into software development, where I approach building interfaces the same way I approach writing music: thoughtfully, deliberately, and with a focus on how everything works together.

Before transitioning into web design, I built a career in the insurance industry, moving from sales into underwriting at Progressive. That experience strengthened my analytical thinking, problem-solving, and ability to work within complex systems—skills I now apply directly to building reliable, user-focused applications.

My shift into frontend development started with a desire for a new challenge and a more creative outlet. As a musician, I was already deeply involved in technical problem-solving: programming MIDI-controlled guitar processors, designing automated live show elements, and building out my own in-ear monitoring system.

After completing a frontend-focused bootcamp, I built a full-featured web application for Skinstric, which includes the use of the selfie camera to analyze users' demographics and dial in the exact skincare product for their needs. That project was a turning point: it showed me I could take an idea and build a complete, working product from the ground up.

Today, I focus on building clean, responsive, and high-performance web experiences using React and Next.js. I enjoy the process of turning ideas into polished products and refining the details that make websites feel intuitive and seamless.

I’ve always been drawn to things that are both technically demanding and creatively expressive—and frontend development sits right at that intersection for me.
